Climatelag

Field: Environmental/Climate Economics | JEL Codes: D91, H41, Q54

Definition

Temporal delay between greenhouse gas emissions and resulting climate impacts creating intertemporal externalities.

Etymology

Compound: 'climate' + 'lag'.

Usage Example

Climatelag explains why democracies struggle with climate policy-today's voters resist immediate costs while future generations bear the consequences of inaction, creating a systematic bias toward delay that accumulates catastrophic risk.

Implementation
